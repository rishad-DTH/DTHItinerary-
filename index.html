Day 01 Sunday, 22 November 2026

Arrival & Into the Wilderness

Colombo Airport — Wilpattu

You arrive at Bandaranaike International Airport in the early hours of the morning aboard your Turkish Airlines flight, where your chauffeur guide is waiting to welcome you before the gentle drive northwards begins. To ease the transition from a long journey, a thoughtful stop has been arranged at Jetwing Blue on the Negombo coast — a calm and elegant setting where you can freshen up, draw breath and enjoy a relaxed snack before continuing towards the wilderness.

From Negombo the route leads inland towards Wilpattu, Sri Lanka's oldest and most storied national park, set within the dry lowlands of the north-west. The landscape gradually transforms around you, coastal palms giving way to open scrub and shaded woodland dotted with the natural sand-rimmed lakes — known locally as villus — that give the park its name and its singular character. The afternoon is left entirely at leisure, allowing you to settle in at Thamaravila, rest properly after the crossing and ease gently into the slower, more elemental rhythm of the days ahead. This measured first day is deliberate — arriving unhurried and refreshed is the finest possible beginning to an extraordinary journey.

Thamaravila Wilpattu

22nd – 24th November · Luxury Tented Chalet · Full Board

Set within seven acres of jungle and just a short drive from the gates of Wilpattu National Park, Thamaravila is an intimate safari lodge of only five elevated tented chalets. Each thousand-square-foot chalet is raised on stilts beneath a thatched roof, with a private open-air deck, a spacious wood-panelled bathroom and an outdoor shower — some including a private plunge pool ideal after a dusty morning in the park. The interiors offer refined, contemporary comfort while blending quietly into the surrounding wilderness, and dining is a highlight, with beautifully presented Sri Lankan and international dishes served in different settings around the grounds, including candlelit dinners on the lawn.

Day 02 Monday, 23 November 2026

Into the Heart of Wilpattu

Full-Day Wildlife Safari

Today is given over fully to the wilderness. An early morning game drive rewards you with the cool light and quiet activity of dawn, when the park is at its most alive and wildlife gathers around the villus to drink. The landscape at this hour — mist rising from the reflective lakes, the call of birds across the canopy — carries a stillness that is deeply affecting. Thamaravila's in-house naturalists will guide your eye and deepen every encounter.

Wilpattu is celebrated above all for its leopards, seen here against a backdrop of open clearings and luminous lakes that lend every sighting a particular sense of space and theatre. Beyond the leopard, the park sustains sloth bear, spotted deer, sambar, wild buffalo, elephant and a wealth of resident and migratory birdlife. Because Wilpattu receives far fewer vehicles than the parks of the south, encounters are unhurried and enjoyed in relative solitude — a quality increasingly rare in the modern safari experience. The afternoon drive offers a second foray into deeper tracts of the park as the heat softens and animals stir once more. You return to the lodge at dusk with the full day's encounters to reflect upon over dinner.

Day 03 Tuesday, 24 November 2026

Eastward to the Cultural Triangle

Wilpattu — Habarana

After an unhurried breakfast at Thamaravila, the journey continues eastward towards Habarana, the verdant heart of Sri Lanka's ancient Cultural Triangle. The drive passes through a gently unscripted panorama of paddy fields gilded by morning light, tank-fed reservoirs shimmering in the distance and rural villages where daily life unfolds at its own unhurried pace — an entirely natural and revealing glimpse of the island beyond the well-worn tourist trail.

Habarana makes an ideal base from which to explore the great monuments and sacred landscapes of the ancient Sinhalese kingdoms, and its central position places the most celebrated highlights within comfortable reach. On arrival, the afternoon is left entirely at leisure, offering time to settle into Taru Villas Maia, to explore the property's tranquil gardens and paddy-field vistas, and to prepare in a spirit of ease for the richer days that follow. The deliberate choice of three nights in one place removes the fatigue of constant movement and allows each day's discoveries to truly settle.

Day 04 Wednesday, 25 November 2026

The Lion Rock at Sunrise

Habarana — Sigiriya — Habarana

The day begins before dawn, with a departure from the hotel at around 5:00 am to climb Sigiriya — the celebrated royal rock fortress that rises, almost impossibly, two hundred metres sheer from the surrounding jungle plain. Reaching the summit as the sun breaks over the horizon is a rare and quietly unforgettable privilege: the early hour brings cooler air, relative solitude and a soft golden wash of light across an immense expanse of forest below. Built in the fifth century by King Kashyapa as an impregnable sky palace, Sigiriya is recognised as a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is one of the finest examples of ancient urban planning in the world, encompassing water gardens, frescoed galleries of remarkable beauty, a mirrored wall and the great lion's paws that guard the final ascent.

After the climb, you return to Taru Villas Maia for a well-earned breakfast and a relaxed morning at leisure beside the pool. As the afternoon light softens, the pace turns gentle once more with a sunset kayak in the company of a local host — gliding quietly across a tank reservoir as the sky deepens into colour, birds come into roost in the trees along the bank, and the day draws to a close in the most graceful manner imaginable.

Day 05 Thursday, 26 November 2026

Village Life & Warm Encounters

Habarana Community Experience

This morning offers one of the most genuinely rewarding experiences of the entire journey: an authentic community engagement programme set within the villages and paddy lands surrounding Habarana. In the easy company of your host Indika, you explore the countryside by whatever means suits your mood — bicycle, e-bike, tuk tuk or jeep — winding along quiet shaded lanes through scenic hamlets where the pace of life has not changed in generations. The countryside itself is extraordinarily beautiful at this hour: water buffalo standing in shallow tanks, women in bright saris crossing paddy paths, the distant sound of temple drums.

The morning unfolds as a series of warm, genuine and entirely unscripted encounters. You witness a temple ceremony of quiet devotion, try your hand at making crispy kokis sweets in a village kitchen, and observe the ancient craft of hand-weaving palm-leaf brooms. There is lively village dance performed with infectious joy, and a visit to Baby Nona — a keeper of traditional herbal oils whose knowledge runs deep. The morning concludes with a homemade Sri Lankan lunch at the Elephant Watch Hut, a meal as delicious as it is heartfelt. The afternoon is yours to enjoy at leisure.

Day 06 Friday, 27 November 2026

The Sacred City of Kandy

Habarana — Kandy

Following a relaxed farewell breakfast, the drive continues south into the rising hill country towards Kandy — the last and most revered capital of the Sinhalese kings, a city of deep cultural and spiritual significance cradled among forested slopes around its calm and beautiful lake. The ascent into the hills brings with it a perceptible change in the air: cooler, greener and more fragrant, the landscape transitioning from the warm plains into the lush tangle of the highlands. On arrival you check in to Taru Villas Kandy and allow the afternoon to open gently around you.

This afternoon brings a Kandy food walk in the engaging company of a local host — an immersive and delicious way to experience the city through its flavours, market stalls and street kitchens, with tastings of the snacks and sweetmeats that define everyday Kandyan life. The walk concludes at the Temple of the Sacred Tooth Relic, the most venerated Buddhist shrine in all of Sri Lanka, which is believed to enshrine a tooth of the Buddha himself. Arriving at dusk, you experience the temple during the evening puja ceremony — the ancient rhythm of drumming, the smoke of incense, the glow of oil lamps and the press of devotion all around you. It is one of those moments that stays long after the journey ends.

Day 07 Saturday, 28 November 2026

Ascending to Little England

Kandy — Nuwara Eliya

Today's drive carries you deeper into the highlands, climbing steadily through some of the most breathtaking scenery on the entire island. The road winds through cascading waterfalls, silver-barked eucalyptus plantations and tea estates etched across the hillsides in every shade of green imaginable. The air grows noticeably cooler as altitude is gained, and the view from the windows shifts with each bend in the road — an ever-changing composition that makes this one of the great scenic drives of Sri Lanka.

Nuwara Eliya sits at the elevated heart of the island's tea country and is fondly known as Little England for its colonial-era bungalows, manicured rose gardens and refreshingly temperate climate — a legacy of the British planters who established this hill station in the nineteenth century. Your stay at Stafford Bungalow, set within a fifty-acre working tea estate some thirty minutes outside town, offers a genuinely atmospheric alternative to conventional hotels. The afternoon is kept deliberately unhurried, allowing the scenic journey to be properly savoured and the cooler highland air to be fully absorbed.

Day 08 Sunday, 29 November 2026

Walking the Pekoe Trail

Stage 22: Pedro to Kandapola

Today you take to the hills on foot, walking Stage 22 of the celebrated Pekoe Trail from Pedro to Kandapola — a route that threads through the very living heart of Ceylon's tea country. Beginning at the iconic Pedro Tea Estate, just three kilometres north of Nuwara Eliya, the path climbs through immaculate estates, fragrant eucalyptus stands and rolling plantation slopes, with sweeping panoramic views across the highlands at every rise. At 2,108 metres above sea level, the trail reaches its highest point, and an optional detour leads to Lover's Leap — a waterfall steeped in a legend of two forbidden lovers whose story has been retold here for generations.

The path winds on past a remote valley village and around the historic Courtlodge Tea Factory, established in 1890 and still working today. Much of this stretch follows exposed estate tracks with little shade, so sun protection is essential — and a warm layer and rain jacket should be kept close, as the highland weather can turn swiftly and dramatically. The trail finishes at Kandapola, where a local tea plucker's lunch awaits along with a cooking demonstration — a deeply satisfying conclusion to a walk that transforms the tea country from a view through a car window into a landscape known first-hand, on foot, in all its detail.

Day 09 Monday, 30 November 2026

The World's Most Beautiful Train Ride

Nuwara Eliya — Ella by Rail

Today brings one of the great railway journeys of the world, as you board the train at Nanu Oya bound for Ella. The line was built by the British in the nineteenth century to carry tea down from the highlands to the coast, and it carries you now through the very heart of the tea country, curving past emerald plantations, fragrant pine forests, tumbling waterfalls and elegant stone viaducts, with the carriage windows framing an ever-changing and endlessly beautiful panorama of the highlands at their most dramatic. This is travel as it was always meant to be: unhurried, scenic and quietly sociable.

The journey takes approximately two and a half hours, and many travellers count it among the most memorable experiences of their entire time in Sri Lanka. As the train descends through the passes and the landscape opens out below, the charming small hill town of Ella announces itself — a place of dramatic views, the famous Nine Arch Bridge, and an atmosphere that combines natural grandeur with a warm, easy-going character all its own. On arrival, a short transfer brings you to 98 Acres Resort & Spa, where tea-estate views and a beautifully positioned hilltop setting await.

Day 10 Tuesday, 01 December 2026

Farewell to the Hills, Hello to the Sea

Ella — Tea Factory — Tangalle

This morning you enjoy a tea factory experience — stepping inside a working estate to witness at first hand how Ceylon's world-famous tea is plucked in the fields above, then withered, rolled, fermented, fired and meticulously graded in the factory below. The process is at once industrial and artisanal, ancient in rhythm yet precise in execution, and the heady, complex fragrance that fills the air of a working factory is something that clings deliciously to the memory long after departure. It is an illuminating and deeply satisfying farewell to the hill country.

From Ella the route descends gradually towards the south coast, the cool highlands yielding to warmer, greener lowland scenery as the journey continues its southward arc to Tangalle — a quiet and beautiful stretch of coastline known for its broad, sweep of beaches, swaying coconut palms and an unhurried pace of life that feels almost entirely undisturbed. This is where the journey turns its register deliberately from exploration to restoration, and you arrive at Kayaam House for a profoundly restful beach and wellness stay at one of Sri Lanka's most exceptional coastal retreats.

Days of Restoration by the Sea

Tangalle — At Leisure

Your days on the south coast are entirely your own. You may choose to spend them in the simplest and most beautiful way imaginable — lingering over breakfast in the dappled morning light, settling beneath the swaying palms with a book as the breeze moves in off the Indian Ocean, or letting the sound of the waves set the rhythm of entire unhurried afternoons. Should you wish to deepen the experience, you may choose to immerse yourself in Kayaam's authentic Ayurvedic wellness programme — a carefully structured approach to inner balance drawing on ancient wisdom, Ayurvedic therapies, natural ingredients and nurturing practice. The programme includes a pre-arrival consultation, a welcome treatment, daily consultations with the resident Ayurvedic doctor, guided wellness meals, morning and evening yoga and meditation classes, and daily use of the steam room, sauna and gym. Whether you choose activity or stillness, these open days at the end of a full and extraordinary journey are entirely intentional — a gift of time to return home truly restored.

A Fond Farewell to the Resplendent Isle

Tangalle — Colombo Airport (BIA)

After a final breakfast and a last few precious moments watching the waves, you are transferred northward to Bandaranaike International Airport in good and comfortable time for your onward flight.
